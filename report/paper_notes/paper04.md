# Main Objective

The paper introduces Tab-VAE, a new Variational Autoencoder specifically designed for synthetic tabular data generation.

Its goal is to improve the generation of mixed numerical and categorical data while overcoming common VAE limitations.

---

# Problem Addressed

Traditional VAEs suffer from Posterior Collapse (Overpruning).

This causes the model to ignore important latent variables, especially when datasets contain many categorical features.

Existing GAN-based models also suffer from:

- unstable training
- mode collapse
- high computational cost

---

# Proposed Solution

The authors propose Tab-VAE.

The model improves standard VAE by introducing:

- Custom feature transformations
- Better handling of numerical variables
- Better handling of categorical variables
- Gumbel-Softmax sampling during inference

---

# Key Concepts

## Variational Autoencoder (VAE)

A VAE learns a compressed latent representation of the original dataset.

After training, new synthetic samples can be generated by sampling from the latent space.

---

## Posterior Collapse

A major problem in VAEs.

The encoder ignores useful latent information because of strong regularization.

As a result:

- poor reconstruction
- weak categorical modeling

---

## Gumbel-Softmax

Instead of always selecting the most probable category,

Tab-VAE samples categories using Gumbel-Softmax.

Benefits:

- better minority class representation
- preserves more information
- reduces information loss

---

# Data Transformation

Continuous variables:

- Variational Gaussian Mixture Model (VGM)
- Mode-specific normalization

Categorical variables:

- One-Hot Encoding

Timestamp variables:

- Sin-Cos transformation

Long-tail distributions:

- Log transformation

# Results

Tab-VAE:

- Outperforms previous VAE models.
- Achieves performance comparable to or better than GAN-based methods.
- Generates higher-quality synthetic tabular data.
- Requires significantly less training time than GANs.
- Approximately 86% faster than the strongest GAN baseline.

---

# Advantages

- Stable training.
- Better categorical modeling.
- Faster training.
- Better minority class generation.
- Less computational cost.
- High-quality synthetic datasets.

---

# Limitations

- Requires careful preprocessing.
- Still depends on hyperparameter tuning.
- Performance may vary depending on dataset characteristics.
